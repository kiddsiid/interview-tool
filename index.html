New here? How to run a Reactioner interview
  1. Set up your slides first. If this cycle is empty, open the ✏️ Slides tab and add your value props (or import a PDF/PowerPoint from the ⚙️ Settings tab).
  2. Pick who you're talking to. Choose a participant slot below and optionally type their real name.
  3. Read each line aloud, in order — the problem, the quote, the promise, each feature, then the price.
  4. Capture their gut reaction. After each line, tap ❤️ (loved it), 👍 (liked it), or 👎 (didn't land). The notes box beside it is private to you.
  5. See what resonated. When you're done, open 🔥 Results for the heatmap, or use 🔗 Share to send the slides to someone to react on their own phone.

Step 1 · Send the slides to your participant

Pick whichever way is easiest for how you're reaching this person.

Their buttons & instructions, plus an auto-translation of your slide wording.

🌐 Slide wording is machine-translated when they open it, so it needs internet and may read a little roughly — especially regional Spanish. The buttons and instructions are professionally translated. Want it exact? Edit the wording yourself after seeing the draft.

📱 Text or email them a link

Best when they're not with you. This makes a link with your slides built in. They tap it on their phone, react, and send the results back.

🤝 Hand them your device (in person)

Best when they're sitting with you. Hand over your laptop or phone — their reactions save automatically. When they hand it back, your results are already filled in.

When they're done, tap your browser's Back button to return here.

Or download a file to send (works offline / no internet needed)

Saves a small file to your computer that you can attach to an email or message. They open it on their phone or laptop and react — no internet required. Handy if a link won't reach them.

Step 2 · Bring their reactions back in

When they finish, they'll get a short code (it starts with RXN-) or a small file. Paste the code or upload the file here, choose who it's from, and their reactions drop straight into your results.

✏️
Each slide is one idea you want to test. Keep it to a few short lines a person can react to. A good slide usually has: a Headline (the problem), a Pain quote (how a real customer describes it), a Promise (the outcome you offer), one or two Features, and a Price. Don't have all of these? That's fine — add only the lines you want, in any order.
